A small RC-controlled pick-and-place build on a mecanum-wheel chassis.
The same line of work on mecanum-wheel holonomic robots at IUB was
later picked up and rebuilt as the
[Holonomic Odometry Testbed](/projects/holonomic-odometry-testbed/) —
the platform behind my 2026 thesis.

A pick-and-place robot built around a mecanum-wheel drivetrain, which
gives it true omnidirectional motion — forward, lateral, and rotation in
place — instead of the constrained kinematics of a differential drive.
The arm picks objects and places them at target positions; navigation is
driven through a PWM RC controller interface.

Built and written up as a coursework project at Independent University,
Bangladesh, and published at the IEEE ICIPRoB 2024 conference in Colombo.
