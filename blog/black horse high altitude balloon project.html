Black Horse Weather Balloon

Back in 2015, I had the privilige to work for a weather balloon launch. The purpose was a media campaign by BlackHorse, a local energy drink company, to send the Bangladeshi Flag as high as possible and I was in charge of Onboard Electronics and Fabrication of the payload.

I made a GPS tracking system for a weather balloon using a Radiometrix NTX-2b, a 433MHz RF transmitter. The mainboard was based on a raspberry Pi, the GPS device used was a Ublox-Neo 6. The onboard camera later recovered gave us some amazing footage.The Recieving Yagi Antenna was made using locally available materials such as copper rods and PVC sheets.

There were multiple attempts and finally the third attempt yielded results after we hacked the GoPro Session. Battery characteristics in extreme temperatures caused the major problems in the first two launches. Finally though, We were able to achieve a height of 1 lac 6 thousand feet.(32.4km)

Data

The vertical distance traversed was 32450.1 meters and the total displacement was 68.8km. The Communication device was able to keep track of it during the full journey while surviving extreme temperatures from - 25.312 Degree Centigrade to 40 Degrees.

The Payload was 500gm with the following content

  • Raspberry Pi 2
  • Communication module with antenna
  • Go Pro Hero Session
  • Primary GPS: Ublox Neo 6M
  • secondary GPS: Spot Tracker
  • Temperature sensors: DS18B20
  • Pressure sensor: BMP185
  • Batteries: Energizer ultimate Lithium
  • Enclosure: Hand carved Styrofoam

We used Helium gas supplied by Linde. Fun fact, pure helium is a very expensive resource in Bangladesh. Most of our resources were exhausted just to pay for the gas. Keeping the payload weight under 500gm was quite a challenge. I don't have access to the press release presentation I made for the campaign along with the raw footage.   • Total Flight Time : 3 Hours 26 Minutes 41 seconds.
  • Total ascend Time: 2 Hours 07 minutes 12 seconds.
  • Total decent Time : 1 hours 19 minutes 29 seconds.
  • Lateral Displacement : 68.8km
  • Lowest recorded outside Temperature: - 25.312 Degree Centigrade
