Graduating Fab Academy

Fab Academy is a prestigious global program which runs in more than 70 Fab Labs, for more than 250 students per year in the largest campus of the world. Fab Academy Program is part of the Academany, the Academy of Almost Anything and taught by the best people in their field. Neil Gerschenfield himself takes the classes every week while local and global instructors provide guidance.

The Fab Academy is where I learnt about all the digital fabrication processes that are available at modern day fabrication labs. Every week I pushed myself to the limit by exploring about a specific topic as much as possible. At the end of the programme I ended up with an aggregate knowledge of design and fabrication of, as the name suggest, 'how to make(almost) anything' by planning and executing a new project each week, resulting in a personal portfolio of technical accomplishments

Fab Academy offers a distributed rather than distance educational model, students learn in local workgroups, with peers, mentors, and machines, which are then connected globally by content sharing and video for interactive classes. I ended up with Ohad Meyuhas from Stratasys as my local instructor while my global instructor was Francisco Sanchez from TECSUP. It was quite an honor learning directly from the legendary Neil Gerschenfield along with my instructors.


What I learnt

The Lessons are divided into weeks with an assignment at the end of the week which will require mastery of that week's topic. Topics ranged from basic web development and version control to laser cutting, CNC machining, PCB Design and Fabrication, 3D Scanning and Printing, Composite Materials, Molding & Casting and so much more along the way!

Everything had to be documented in order to pass the assignments. At the end of the program, I ended up with a treasure trove of documentation. Fab Academy Documentation

Machine Design Project

The machine design week was pretty chaotic, it was a group project between five of us from completely different backgrounds where we managed to intertwine our best attributes to make a 'Machine'!

I was incharge of the electronics and embedded programming but that quickly escalated to CAD for the mechanism as well, but we managed to pull of this amazing victorian era inspired mechanism for a new take at a Marble Maze.
